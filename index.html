How it works

Three questions.
Thirty seconds.
One focused day.

Before you act, you think. ARETE walks you through the only three questions that matter for any meaningful task — eliminating the friction that causes avoidance before it ever starts.

Step 1 — WHY

Why does this matter?

The clearer your reason, the less likely you are to avoid the task when resistance hits. Purpose is procrastination’s antidote.

Step 2 — HOW

How will you do it?

Defining your approach before you start removes the friction that causes procrastination. You’ve already solved the hard part before you begin.

Step 3 — FULFILLMENT

What makes it worth doing?

Even hard tasks become easier when the meaning is clear. Connect your effort to what matters most to you — not just the outcome.

Features

Everything you need.
Nothing you don’t.

ARETE is intentionally minimal. Every feature earns its place by making you more effective, not just more productive.

Weekly AI Insights

At the end of each week, ARETE’s AI reviews your reflections and surfaces real patterns — which tasks energized you, which drained you, and where your effort is paying off. Not generic tips. Your story, told back to you.

Voice Input

Record your approach or purpose hands-free using Whisper AI. Capture your thinking the moment it happens — without breaking your flow or opening a keyboard.

Work & Personal

Two focused lists, each with their own 2–3 task intention. Separate your work and personal tasks without the cognitive cost of mixing contexts in the same view.

Focus Streak

A streak that rewards showing up with intention, not volume. Complete your chosen tasks for five consecutive days and your streak grows — building a practice, not a habit of adding more.

The science behind ARETE

Built on what researchers
know about follow-through.

ARETE’s three-question structure isn’t a hunch. It’s grounded in decades of peer-reviewed research on motivation, procrastination, and habit formation.

“

People who form implementation intentions — specifying when, where, and how they will act — are 2–3 times more likely to follow through on their goals than those who only state intent.

Gollwitzer & Sheeran, 2006 — Meta-analysis of 94 studies on implementation intentions. Psychological Bulletin, 132(4), 536–575.
“

When people understand the personal significance of their work, they show greater intrinsic motivation, persistence, and wellbeing — regardless of task difficulty or external reward.

Deci & Ryan, 2000 — Self-determination theory and the facilitation of intrinsic motivation, social development, and wellbeing. Psychological Inquiry, 11(4), 227–268.
“

After a task interruption, it takes an average of 23 minutes and 15 seconds to fully return to the original task. Limiting task count directly reduces this cognitive switching cost.

Mark, Gonzalez & Harris, 2005 — No task left behind: Examining the nature of fragmented work. Proceedings of CHI 2005, ACM Press.
“

Progress on meaningful goals — even small wins — is the single most important factor in daily inner work life: motivation, emotions, and creative output.

Amabile & Kramer, 2011 — The power of small wins. Harvard Business Review. Based on 12,000 diary entries across 238 professionals in 7 companies.
